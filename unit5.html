üìè A. Atomic Radius

Distance from nucleus to outermost shell.

  • Across a period: Decreases (‚Üë nuclear charge pulls electrons closer)
  • Down a group: Increases (new shells added)
Trend Explanation

‚ö° B. Ionization Energy (IE)

Energy required to remove one electron from a gaseous atom.

  • Across a period: Increases (‚Üë nuclear attraction, electrons held tightly)
  • Down a group: Decreases (outer electrons farther from nucleus)

üß™ Noble gases have highest IE; Alkali metals have lowest.

üí° C. Electronegativity

Tendency of an atom to attract shared electrons in a bond.

  • Across a period: Increases (non-metals attract electrons more)
  • Down a group: Decreases (shielding increases, less pull)

Highest: Fluorine (F) | Lowest: Cesium (Cs)
