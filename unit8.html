üîπ1. What is a Chemical Reaction?

A chemical reaction is a process in which one or more substances (reactants) are transformed into new substances (products) with different properties.

üß™ Example:
2H2 + O2 ‚Üí 2H2O

üß† Balanced Equation Example:

üîπ3. Types of Chemical Reactions

  • üî∏ A. Combination Reaction
    Two or more reactants combine to form a single product.
    General form: A + B ‚Üí AB
    üß™ Example: CaO + H2O ‚Üí Ca(OH)2
  • üî∏ B. Decomposition Reaction
    A single compound breaks down into two or more products.
    General form: AB ‚Üí A + B
    Types: Thermal (by heat), Electrolytic (by electricity), Photolytic (by light)
    üß™ Example: 2Pb(NO3)2 ‚Üíheat 2PbO + 4NO2 + O2
  • üî∏ C. Displacement Reaction
    A more reactive element displaces a less reactive element from its compound.
    General form: A + BC ‚Üí AC + B
    üß™ Example: Zn + CuSO4 ‚Üí ZnSO4 + Cu
  • üî∏ D. Double Displacement Reaction
    Two compounds exchange ions to form two new compounds.
    General form: AB + CD ‚Üí AD + CB
    üß™ Example: Na2SO4 + BaCl2 ‚Üí BaSO4‚Üì + 2NaCl
    Note: Often leads to precipitation, gas evolution, or neutralization.

    • üîπ4. Redox Reactions

    üìò Oxidation

    • Loss of electrons
    • Gain of oxygen or loss of hydrogen

    üìò Reduction

    • Gain of electrons
    • Loss of oxygen or gain of hydrogen

    üîÅ Redox Reaction = Oxidation + Reduction occur simultaneously
    üß™ Example: CuO + H2 ‚Üí Cu + H2O
    CuO is reduced to Cu; H2 is oxidized to H2O.

    üîπ5. Exothermic and Endothermic Reactions

  • üî• Exothermic Reaction
    Releases energy (usually heat), temperature increases. Most combustion and neutralization reactions.
    üß™ Example: C + O2 ‚Üí CO2 + Heat
  • ‚ùÑÔ∏è Endothermic Reaction
    Absorbs energy, temperature decreases. Often involves photosynthesis or decomposition.
    üß™ Example: CaCO3 ‚Üíheat CaO + CO2

    • üéØKey Concepts to Master
